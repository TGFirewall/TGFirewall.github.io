THE GREAT FIREWALL

THE CULTURAL IMPACT OF OPERATION GOLDEN SHIELD

Abstract:

Internet access is an increasingly important part of daily life around the world, and China is no exception. Chinese citizens comprise a massive portion of the world’s internet users, numbering over 700 million in 2016. However, Chinese citizens’ access to the internet is restricted by a massively complex system of controls known as the Great Firewall to block information that the Chinese government does not want its citizens to access. This portfolio examines the cultural impact of the Great Firewall and government censorship on the Chinese people through technical, historical, and contemporary lenses. After an introduction to the Great Firewall and Operation Golden Shield, the article is comprised of three portions, and includes background readings, information, and gathered material. The first section frames the modern issue of internet censorship in the historical and political contexts of ancient Chinese imperial practice and modern Communist policies. The second explains how the Chinese government implements internet censorship through government agencies and the technical means used to achieve that end. The third section examines how the Great Firewall culturally sandboxes Chinese internet users away from the rest of the internet, and looks at the subculture that has developed within, including linguistic connections, mobile phone culture, and how effective the government really is at restricting internet access.
